MARIAN APPARITIONS

Our Lady of Guadalupe

Years: 1531

Seers: Juan Diego Cuauhtlatoatzin, Juan Bernardino

Location: Tepeyac, Mexico (Spanish colonial empire)

Approval: 1555

Feast: December 12

Description: Juan Diego, a recently converted Aztec, saw an apparition of Mary on December 9, 1531, who asked for a church to be built on the site. When the local bishop doubted him, Mary appeared again and left her image on his cloak. This cloak is now displayed at the Basilica of Our Lady of Guadalupe in Mexico City. Over time, Our Lady of Guadalupe became a symbol of Catholic faith in Mexico and among the Mexican diaspora.

Our Lady of the Miraculous Medal / Our Lady of Graces

Years: 1830

Seers: Catherine Labouré

Location: Rue du Bac in Paris, France

Approval: 1836

Feast: November 27

Description: Catherine Labouré, a novice with the Daughters of Charity, reported Marian apparitions in 1830. In one, Mary told her that "times were evil" and warned of future sorrows. On November 27, Mary showed her a vision and asked for a medal to be made, promising great graces to those who wore it. The medal, first produced in 1832, became known as the Miraculous Medal due to the miracles associated with it. Its design features Mary, a cross, the letter M, and the Sacred and Immaculate Hearts.

Our Lady of La Salette

Years: 1846

Seers: Mélanie Calvat, Maximin Giraud

Location: Mount Sous-Les Baisses, La Salette-Fallavaux, France

Approval: 1851

Feast: September 19

Description:Two shepherd children reported seeing an apparition of Mary, who was weeping because of disrespect shown to the Lord's Day and to the Lord's name. She entrusted one secret to each of the two children, which they wrote down and delivered to Pope Pius IX.

Our Lady of Lourdes - The Immaculate Conception

Years: 1858

Seers: Bernadette Soubirous

Location: Lourdes, France

Approval: 1862

Feast: February 11

Description: Fourteen-year-old Bernadette Soubirous reported seeing Mary while gathering firewood. Mary called herself "the Immaculate Conception" and revealed a spring with healing powers. Since 1862, over sixty cures linked to Lourdes have been declared miraculous by the Church, which created a Medical Bureau in 1883 to evaluate such claims. A 2012 study called some cures "impressive" and still unexplained by science.

Our Lady of Knock

Years: 1879

Seers: Group of 15 people

Location: Knock, Ireland

Approval: 1879

Feast: August 17

Description: On August 21, 1879, fifteen people in Knock, Ireland, saw a silent apparition behind their church: an altar with a lamb (symbolizing Jesus), surrounded by angels, with Mary, Joseph, and St. John nearby. Lasting 90 minutes, the vision is considered Marian but is unique for including multiple figures and having no spoken message.

Our Lady of Fátima / Our Lady of the Rosary - The Immaculate Heart of Mary

Years: 1917

Seers: Lúcia Santos, Jacinta Marto, Francisco Marto

Location: Cova da Iria, Fátima, Portugal

Approval: 1930

Feast: May 13

Description: Three shepherd children in Fátima, Portugal, reported seeing an angel, followed by apparitions of Mary, who revealed three secrets involving hell, future global upheavals, and persecution of the Church. The events culminated in the Miracle of the Sun, witnessed by around 70,000 people. All popes since have affirmed belief in the apparitions. Pope John Paul II credited Our Lady of Fátima with saving his life after a 1981 assassination attempt. In 1925, Sister Lúcia reported related apparitions, known as the Pontevedra apparitions.
